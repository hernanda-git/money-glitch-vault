> "Keadaan ekonomi Indonesia sebetulnya secara fundamental cukup baik. Namun kita memang menghadapi pelemahan rupiah yang lebih daripada peers kita. Berarti kita juga harus mewaspadai bagaimana menjaga isu confidence dan trust," kata Mari Elka Pangestu, Wakil Ketua DEN.

## Evidence of volume
- IHSG down 31.81% year-to-date as of 26 June 2026, one of worst performers in ASEAN
- Foreign net sell reached Rp71.68 trillion, massive capital flight from Indonesian markets
- BEI (stock exchange) publicly acknowledging need to rebuild investor confidence
- IPOT launching AI tools specifically because retail investors cannot interpret market data
- Rupiah weakening more than peer countries, indicating Indonesia-specific confidence crisis

## Existing solutions (and why they fail)
- Solution A: Reksadana (mutual funds) — fails because fund managers also selling, creating correlated downside; retail investors lose trust in intermediaries
- Solution B: Edukasi pasar modal via sekuritas — fails because education does not address the structural problem of foreign outflow and currency weakness
- Solution C: BEI reformasi pasar modal — announced but no concrete timeline; retail investors need immediate help, not years of reform
- Solution D: IPOT AI Analytics — useful but only helps with stock picking, does not solve macro-level capital flight or currency risk

## Your wedge
Build a portfolio protection tool for Indonesian retail investors that automatically hedges against rupiah depreciation and foreign outflow using simple, low-cost instruments. Currently, retail investors have no access to currency hedging or macro-risk protection tools that institutional investors use. A mobile-first app that: (1) monitors foreign flow data in real-time, (2) automatically shifts portfolio to defensive sectors (consumer staples, utilities) when foreign outflow exceeds threshold, (3) offers simple gold-backed or dollar-denominated micro-investments as hedge. The wedge is simplicity: make macro hedging accessible to anyone with Rp 100,000, not just institutions with Rp 10 billion.
