## Existing solutions (and why they fail)
- Solution A: IndiHome/broadband consumer packages — fails because these are designed for home use, not business; shared bandwidth causes slow speeds during peak hours
- Solution B: iForte Sonic (semi-dedicated) — promising but likely expensive for micro UMKM earning Rp 3-5 juta/bulan; CIR 1:2 ratio still shared
- Solution C: Starlink/satellite internet — not yet available in Indonesia at affordable price points; coverage gaps remain in eastern Indonesia
- Solution D: Government USO (Universal Service Obligation) programs — slow rollout, bureaucratic, often installed but not maintained

## Your wedge
Build a community internet cooperative for UMKM clusters in tier-2 and tier-3 cities where commercial ISPs underserve. The model: 20-50 UMKM in a pasar (traditional market) or ruko (shophouse) cluster pool Rp 200,000-500,000/bulan each to fund a shared high-speed fiber connection with local managed WiFi. A lightweight management app handles: (1) bandwidth allocation per member, (2) automatic payment collection via QRIS, (3) basic network monitoring, (4) usage analytics. The wedge is that these UMKM are already physically clustered in pasar or ruko rows, making shared infrastructure economically viable where individual connections are not. Think "PLN for internet" at the neighborhood level.

## What people would pay
- Rp 200,000-500,000 per bulan per UMKM for guaranteed business-grade internet
- Evidence: iForte Sonic targets this exact segment, proving willingness to pay; current UMKM pay Rp 150,000-300,000 for unreliable consumer broadband
- Comparable: IndiHome business packages start at Rp 350,000/bulan for 30 Mbps; community model could deliver 100 Mbps shared at similar cost per member
