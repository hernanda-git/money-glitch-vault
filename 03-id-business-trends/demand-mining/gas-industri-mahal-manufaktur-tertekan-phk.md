## The pain (verbatim quotes in Indonesian)
> "Pada tahun 2025, volume Gas Bumi Tertentu yang diterima oleh sektor industri sementara ini tercatat baru berkisar 60 persen hingga 70 persen dari alokasi yang telah ditetapkan secara sah di dalam Keputusan Menteri ESDM Nomor 76K/2025. Terjadi kesenjangan yang lebar antara regulasi di atas kertas dengan komitmen pasokan fisik dari produsen gas di lapangan," ujar Febri Hendri Antoni Arif, Juru Bicara Kementerian Perindustrian.
> "Kami sudah lapor bapak presiden, dan sudah dihitung, resmi gas untuk industri harganya turun menjadi 13 Dolar AS per MMBtu," kata Bahlil Lahadalia, Menteri ESDM.
> "Penurunan harga gas bukan sekadar urusan angka di atas kertas, melainkan menyangkut hajat hidup orang banyak, terutama para buruh yang nasibnya bergantung pada keberlangsungan operasional pabrik," ujar Sufmi Dasco Ahmad, Wakil Ketua DPR RI.

## Evidence of volume
- Kemenperin confirms gas allocation only realized 60-70% of what was mandated by regulation
- Multiple industry associations (keramik, otomotif, tekstil) publicly threatened PHK
- Serikat pekerja from multiple factories organized protests demanding gas price cuts
- The issue was escalated to presidential level, indicating scale of crisis
- At least 5 major news outlets covered this story on the same day (29 June 2026)

## Existing solutions (and why they fail)
- Solution A: AGIT (Alokasi Gas Industri Tertentu) policy — fails because allocation is only realized 60-70%, gas producers do not deliver promised volumes
- Solution B: HGBT (Harga Gas Bumi Tertentu) regulation — fails because price still too high at USD 20-23 per MMBtu before intervention, and production in Java declining
- Solution C: Government intervention to cut price to USD 13 — may work short-term but does not solve underlying supply shortage and declining gas production in Java

## Your wedge
Build a gas procurement platform for UMKM manufacturers that aggregates demand across hundreds of small factories to negotiate better rates directly with gas producers. Currently, small manufacturers have zero bargaining power and pay retail prices while large corporates get subsidized rates. A cooperative-style platform could pool demand from 100+ small ceramic, textile, or food processing factories in industrial clusters (like Sidoarjo or Tangerang) to negotiate bulk gas supply contracts at rates closer to what large corporates pay. Think "Tokopedia for industrial gas" where small manufacturers collectively bid for supply. The wedge is that these factories are already clustered geographically, making logistics feasible.

## What people would pay
- Rp 5-15 juta per bulan per pabrik for guaranteed gas supply at 20-30% below current spot prices
- Evidence: The price differential between subsidized gas (USD 13) and market rate (USD 20-23) is ~40%, meaning factories would gladly pay a platform fee to access lower rates
- Comparable: Large corporates already pay dedicated gas suppliers like Pertamina Gas or PT Saka Energi for bulk contracts; small manufacturers are excluded from these arrangements

## Adjacent opportunities
- Energy audit services for UMKM manufacturers to reduce gas consumption
- Alternative energy sourcing (biogas, solar thermal) for factories in gas-deprived areas
- Equipment retrofitting to convert gas-dependent processes to electricity (especially with new lower industrial electricity tariffs)
- Insurance products for supply chain disruption in manufacturing
